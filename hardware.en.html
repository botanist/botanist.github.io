Gateway. Connecting to our service

In order for our sensors and actors to be able to communication with botani.st a gateway is needed which bridges the RF we use with internet. Our gateway connects to your local network via either Wi-Fi or a network cable (ethernet / RJ-45).

If it looses contact with our service a built-in memory stores all data and automatically synchronizes it once it regains contact with our servers.

You can add multiple gateways to your account. This can be handy if you have thick concrete walls that severely reduces our RF signal or want to reach distant parts where our RF doesn't reach.

A built-in hub with 4 ports to which you can connect sensors and actors makes is cheap and easy to get started.

Hubs. Connect your wired sensors wirelessly

Our hubs provides wireless connectivity for our wired sensors and actors. With a powerful battery and solar cells for charging it can handle many devices.

If the built-in battery isn't enough there's a micro-USB port that you can use a normal cellphone charger with. Our hubs are designed to handle up to 64 devices each.

Wireless sensor. Maximum freedom of placement

Our basic sensor measures moisture level in the soil, temperature in both air and soil as well as ambient light. Moisture is read at about 7 cm depth with 3-5% accuracy thru a capacitive method. It means that the amount of nutrients or salts in the soil doesn't affect the reading. Temperature readings are exact to ±0.4°C.

The wireless sensor comes with a built-in battery and solar cells for recharging. They are waterproof and withstands normal rain and watering without any problem.

An LED on the device shows the current status - green if everything is ok, orange if something small is wrong or red if there's an emergency that needs fixing.

With a size of approx 40 x 30 x 10 mm it's non-intrusive in most pots.

Wired sensor. Smaller and cheaper

Our wired basic sensor is just as capable as its wireless counterpart. In fact it's the same moisture and temperature sensor. With the absence of radio, battery and solar cells we are able to cut down not only on size but also price significantly.

With a size of approx 25 x 25 x 7 mm it's almost invisible in your greenhouse. About 2 meters of cable give you plenty of range to the nearest hub or another sensor as you can connect these in a daisy chain.

Smart outlet. Turn on a fan, irrigation or extra lighting

Our smart power outlet can be controlled from botani.st when for example a sensor thinks it's to warm, the soil is too dry or other events. It handles 230 V / 13A and is of course grounded.
